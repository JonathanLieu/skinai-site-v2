What we catch

The conflicts hiding
in your bathroom cabinet

Most people have at least one product conflict they don't know about. These are the most common ones we detect.

High impact
Vitamin C + Retinol

These ingredients work best at different pH levels. Layering them at the same time can reduce the effectiveness of both.

Use Vitamin C in the morning, retinol at night. Simple split, full benefit.
High impact
AHA/BHA + Retinol

Both are powerful exfoliants. Using them together — especially when you're starting out — can overwhelm your skin barrier and cause irritation.

Alternate nights until your skin builds tolerance. Start slow.
Medium impact
High-dose Vitamin C + Strong Acids

Vitamin C (L-ascorbic acid) at high concentrations layered with other low-pH actives can cause irritation and reduce stability of both products.

Use vitamin C alone in the morning. Save other actives for PM.
High impact
Benzoyl Peroxide + Retinol

Benzoyl peroxide can degrade some retinol formulations when applied together. The fix is simple — don't layer them at the same time.

Use BP in the morning, retinol at night. Modern formulations handle this well.
