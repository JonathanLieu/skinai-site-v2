Ingredient conflicts

The conflicts hiding
in your cabinet

87% of our beta users had at least one harmful ingredient conflict in their existing routine. Most had two or three. These are the most common ones our engine detects automatically — and what to do about them.

Skincare conflicts aren't just about ingredients "not working" — many combinations actively damage your skin barrier, accelerate degradation of active ingredients, or cause inflammation you might mistake for sensitivity. Here's what we catch:

High impact

Vitamin C + Retinol

Together they oxidize and deactivate each other. You're paying for two premium products that cancel out every night they're used together.

✓ Use Vitamin C AM, retinol PM. Never together.
High impact

AHA/BHA + Retinol

Both break down skin cells at the cellular level. Combined on the same night, you're over-exfoliating and actively compromising your skin barrier.

✓ Alternate nights. Never on the same evening.
Low impact

Vitamin C + Niacinamide

Modern research shows they are safe for most. However, in sensitive skin, the low pH of Vitamin C can convert Niacinamide into niacin, causing temporary flushing.

✓ Safe to layer. If flushing occurs, separate by 15 minutes.
High impact

Benzoyl Peroxide + Retinol

Benzoyl peroxide oxidizes retinol and renders it completely inactive. If you're using both, one of them is doing absolutely nothing.

✓ Use BP in the AM, retinol PM. Completely separate.
Medium impact

Multiple AHAs simultaneously

Glycolic, lactic, and mandelic acid all exfoliate in slightly different ways. Stacking them disrupts your barrier and causes chronic over-exfoliation.

✓ Choose one acid per routine. Rotate weekly, not nightly.
High impact

Copper Peptides + Vitamin C

Copper ions catalyze the oxidation of Vitamin C, rendering it completely ineffective almost instantly. They also destabilize the peptide's ability to repair skin.

✓ Use Vitamin C AM, Copper Peptides PM. Never layer.
Low impact

Peptides + AHAs/BHAs

Acids lower your skin's pH, which can denature (unfold) delicate peptide chains before they penetrate. You lose nearly all of the anti-aging benefits of the peptide.

✓ Apply peptides on alternate nights from exfoliants.
High impact

Salicylic Acid + Drying Actives

Stacking BHA with alcohol-heavy toners or benzoyl peroxide strips the skin far faster than the barrier can regenerate, leading to reactive oiliness.

✓ One drying active per session. Balance with ceramides.
Safe pairings

Synergy: What works
better together

Vitamin C + Vitamin E + Ferulic

The gold standard. Ferulic acid doubles the photoprotective effect of Vitamin C.

Retinol + Niacinamide

Niacinamide strengthens the barrier, reducing the typical irritation from retinol.

Hyaluronic Acid + Everything

The ultimate buffer. Use it to hydrate before applying stronger actives.

Ceramides + Exfoliants

Always follow AHAs with ceramides to patch the barrier you just opened.
